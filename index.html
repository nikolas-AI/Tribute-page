Nikola Tesla "Revolutionizing Electricity and Pioneering AC Power Systems"
Illuminating the World with Electricity
Nikola Tesla, the Serbian-American inventor, electrical engineer, and futurist, revolutionized electricity through his pioneering work in AC power systems, particularly the invention of the AC induction motor, laying the foundation for modern power distribution and numerous technological advancements.
Name: Nikola Tesla
Birthdate: July 10, 1856
Birthplace: Smiljan, Croatia (then part of the Austrian Empire)
Nationality: Serbian-American
Occupation: Inventor, Electrical Engineer, Futurist
Notable Contributions:
Pioneered alternating current (AC) power systems
Invented the AC induction motor
Developed the Tesla coil for high-voltage transformation
Explored wireless power transmission and communication
Education:
Technical University of Graz, Austria
Major Inventions:
AC induction motor
Tesla coil
Tesla turbine
Legacy:
Revolutionized power distribution
Contributed to the development of modern electrical systems
Influenced the fields of engineering and technology
Considered one of the greatest inventors in history
Date of Death: January 7, 1943
Place of Death: New York City, United States
Age at Death: 86
